## Platform Summaries

### Kit

**URL:** kit.com | **Affiliate:** ✅ Active

**What it is:** Creator-first email platform. Rebuilt from ConvertKit (rebranded 2024). Focused on newsletter operators, digital product sellers, podcasters, coaches, and anyone monetizing a personal brand audience.

**Pricing:**

| Plan | Monthly | Contacts |
|------|---------|----------|
| Newsletter (Free) | $0 | ~10,000 |
| Creator | $33/mo | Unlimited |
| Pro | $66/mo | Unlimited |

Scales with list size on Creator/Pro — at 10,000 subscribers, Creator is ~$100/mo.

**Where Kit wins:**
- Best-in-class creator monetization: sell digital products, paid newsletters, and subscriptions natively
- Genuinely useful free tier (~10,000 subs, unlimited broadcasts, 1 automation)
- Subscriber Signals (Pro): AI-powered audience intelligence on real people behind email addresses
- Built-in newsletter referral system (Pro) — equivalent to $99/mo SparkLoop
- Strong deliverability reputation

**Honest weaknesses:**
- Bills on total subscribers including inactive contacts — cold contacts still cost you
- 3.5% + 30¢ transaction fee on ALL product sales, including free plan
- No email heatmaps on any plan
- Advanced reporting locked to Pro — Creator plan users fly partially blind
- At 55K subs on Creator: $316/mo — Moosend handles similar volume at $320/mo with more e-comm features
- Only 1 automation and 1 sequence on free — real funnels need Creator

**Best for:** Newsletter creators, solo content creators, podcasters, course sellers, coaches, authors. Any individual building a personal brand audience.

**Not for:** E-commerce brands, B2B teams needing CRM, agencies, large businesses needing deep reporting.

### Moosend

**URL:** moosend.com | **Affiliate:** ✅ Active

**What it is:** Value-positioned email automation platform. Known for delivering enterprise-grade automation features at SMB prices. Customers include Vogue, Domino's, and WWF.

**Pricing:**

| Plan | Monthly | Contacts |
|------|---------|----------|
| Free Trial | $0 | 1,000 (30 days only) |
| Pro | From $7/mo | 500+ (scales) |
| Moosend+ | Custom | Custom |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

Active-subscriber billing: charges based on engaged contacts (~6-month activity window), not total list size.

| Subscribers (Pro) | Monthly |
|-------------------|---------|
| Up to 1,000 | $7 |
| 2,000 | $9 |
| 5,000 | $32 |
| 10,000 | $80 |
| 25,000 | $160 |

**Where Moosend wins:**
- Best price-to-feature ratio in the comparison — unlimited automation from $7/mo
- Engaged-only billing: 40–60% real-world savings vs. platforms that bill on total list
- Email heatmaps included on all paid plans (most competitors charge extra or don't offer it)
- AI-powered product recommendations built into paid plans
- E-commerce automation: cart abandonment, product view triggers, purchase sequences
- No monthly email send caps — unlimited sends on all plans
- Pay-as-you-go credit option for seasonal senders

**Honest weaknesses:**
- No free plan — trial expires after 30 days
- Auto-renewing annual contracts — cancellation friction reported by multiple users
- Transactional email is a paid add-on (Moosend+), not included in Pro
- No SMS marketing
- Smaller template library than Mailchimp
- Support quality thins out for Pro users vs. Enterprise
- Salesforce/complex CRM integrations require Zapier (~$20–50/mo extra)

**Best for:** E-commerce SMBs, digital marketers wanting automation depth at budget prices, Shopify/WooCommerce stores needing cart recovery and product recommendations.

**Not for:** Pure newsletter/creator use cases (Kit is better), enterprises needing SSO and SLA included, teams needing transactional email in base plan.

### Mailchimp

**URL:** mailchimp.com | **Affiliate:** 🔜 Planned (no active affiliate relationship)

**What it is:** The most recognized name in email marketing. Owned by Intuit. Largest user base globally. Known for ease of use and template library. Has made significant pricing changes since eliminating its generous free plan in 2019.

**Pricing (dynamic — confirm at mailchimp.com/pricing):**

| Plan | Starting Price | Contact Limit |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | **250 contacts** |
| Essentials | ~$13/mo | 50,000 |
| Standard | ~$20/mo | 100,000 |
| Premium | ~$350/mo | Unlimited |

Prices scale with list size. Standard at 25,000 contacts is approximately $230/mo.

**Where Mailchimp wins:**
- Brand recognition — most non-technical clients have heard of it
- Largest email template library in the industry
- Best beginner UX — easiest to learn for first-time email marketers
- Native Shopify, WooCommerce, and Stripe integrations
- SMS marketing available (Standard+) — only platform in this roundup with native SMS
- Generative AI features included on Standard+

**Honest weaknesses:**
- **Free tier: 250 contacts.** Not a real free plan — more of a demo
- **Essentials automation: 4 flow steps.** Not enough for any real nurture sequence
- **Trustpilot: ~1.5/5** — documented patterns of account suspension, billing after cancellation, unresponsive support at scale
- Bills on total contacts including unsubscribed if not manually purged; counts duplicates across audiences separately
- Monthly email send caps (10x/12x contact count)
- Pricing has increased repeatedly — legacy users often have different rates than new signups
- Free plan support: none after 30 days
- Among the most expensive per-subscriber cost at mid-list sizes (5K–25K)

**Best for:** True beginners who need the easiest possible start, small businesses where Mailchimp's brand name provides client credibility, businesses specifically needing SMS + email in one tool.

**Not for:** Budget-conscious businesses, power automation users, businesses with growing lists (pricing becomes painful), anyone who has read the Trustpilot reviews and needs confidence in billing reliability.

### GetResponse

**URL:** getresponse.com | **Affiliate:** 🔜 Application pending

**What it is:** All-in-one marketing suite established 1998 (Gdańsk, Poland). Email is the core, but GetResponse differentiates with built-in webinar hosting, course creator, website builder, and sales funnels. Positioned for coaches, educators, and marketers who want multiple tools consolidated.

**Pricing (verified June 14, 2026 at getresponse.com/pricing — 1,000 contacts):**

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per mo) |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Starter | $19 | $15.58 |
| Marketer | $59 | $48.38 |
| Creator | $69 | $56.58 |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

Annual billing saves 18%. No free plan — 14-day trial only.

**Where GetResponse wins:**
- Best all-in-one value: email + funnels + webinars + courses + website builder + push notifications in one subscription
- Webinar hosting native: up to 100 attendees (Creator) or 1,000 (Enterprise) — no separate Zoom/WebinarJam needed
- Course creator with 0% transaction fees — no commission on course revenue
- Sales funnel builder included — no separate ClickFunnels subscription needed
- Premium newsletter monetization (charge subscribers directly, Substack-style)
- Automation depth at Marketer level ($59/mo): unlimited workflows, cart recovery, behavioral triggers

**Honest weaknesses:**
- **Starter automation: 1 workflow only.** Want a welcome sequence AND a re-engagement sequence? You need Marketer at $59/mo — a $40/mo jump from Starter
- No free plan — 14-day trial only, and time your decision accordingly
- Interface complexity — so many features creates genuine UX friction and a steeper learning curve than Kit or Mailchimp
- SMS and transactional email: Enterprise only — not available at lower tiers
- Webinar recording limited on Creator: 3 hours. Most webinar platforms give more. Enterprise gets 20 hours
- Templates feel dated compared to Kit or Mailchimp aesthetics
- AI features limited on Starter (3 uses each for AI landing page creator and subject line generator)
- Not ideal if you just need email — you're paying for webinar and course infrastructure you may never use

**Best for:** Coaches, consultants, online educators who want email + webinars + courses + funnels under one roof. E-commerce businesses on Marketer tier needing full automation with abandoned cart and purchase follow-up.

**Not for:** Pure email marketers who want simplicity, enterprise companies needing deep CRM (ActiveCampaign wins), ultra-budget users (Moosend is cheaper for comparable email automation).

### ActiveCampaign

**URL:** activecampaign.com | **Affiliate:** ❌ Competitor — no affiliate relationship. Included for comparison value only.

**What it is:** The most sophisticated marketing automation platform in the mid-market segment. PCMag: "An Automation Powerhouse for Data-Driven Email Marketing." Has the deepest visual automation builder, built-in CRM with deal pipeline, and machine learning-powered sending optimization.

**Pricing (estimates from verified review sources — confirm at activecampaign.com/pricing):**

| Plan | Monthly (est.) | Annual (est.) | Contacts |
|------|---------------|--------------|---------|
| Starter | ~$15/mo | ~$9/mo | 1,000 |
| Plus | ~$49/mo | ~$30/mo | 1,000 |
| Professional | ~$79/mo | ~$49/mo | 1,000 |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Custom |

No free plan. 14-day trial. Annual billing ~40–50% cheaper than monthly.

**Where ActiveCampaign wins:**
- Best visual automation builder in the industry — conditional logic, split paths, goal-based exits, custom events, date triggers, behavioral tracking
- Machine learning predictive sending — optimizes send time per individual contact
- Built-in CRM with deal pipeline, lead scoring, contact notes — eliminates need for separate CRM for many B2B teams
- Win probability (Professional) — ML-predicts deal close likelihood; valuable for sales-led businesses
- Deepest segmentation of any platform here: behavioral, purchase, engagement, custom events, geolocation
- Salesforce integration (Professional) — bidirectional sync, one of the most complete available

**Honest weaknesses:**
- Gets expensive fast at scale: 10,000 contacts at Professional = ~$186/mo
- Steepest learning curve of all platforms reviewed — overwhelming for small teams without dedicated marketing staff
- No free plan at all
- Email template design is weaker than Mailchimp or GetResponse — less polished aesthetics
- No native webinar or course delivery features (unlike GetResponse)
- CRM features locked to Plus ($49/mo+) — if that's the main reason you're considering it, budget for it
- Trustpilot ~3.2/5 — complaints about pricing, billing, and learning curve
- Overkill for simple newsletter or basic nurture use cases — you pay for power you may not use

**Best for:** B2B businesses, sales-led organizations, e-commerce companies with complex multi-step customer journeys, marketing teams with 1,000–50,000 contacts and dedicated staff to manage the platform.

**Not for:** Beginners, solo creators, budget-sensitive businesses, anyone who just needs newsletters and simple automation.

## Recommendations by Use Case

### Creator / Newsletter Operator

**Winner: Kit**

Starting cost: $0 (free up to ~10,000 subs) | First paid tier: $33/mo

Kit's creator monetization ecosystem — digital products, paid newsletters, built-in referral system — has no equivalent here. The free tier is genuinely useful. Honest caveat: transaction fees of 3.5% + 30¢ apply to all sales on all plans, and inactive subscriber billing inflates costs at scale.

---

### Small Business (General — Under 10K Contacts)

**Winner: Moosend**

Starting cost: $7/mo

Full automation, unlimited sends, heatmaps, A/B testing, and e-commerce tracking at $7/mo. Active-subscriber billing means real-world cost is often 30–50% lower than competitors. Caveats: no free plan, watch the auto-renewing annual contract.

---

### E-Commerce Store (Shopify/WooCommerce)

**Winner: Moosend** (under 25K contacts) | **ActiveCampaign** (large, complex journeys)

Moosend at $7–$160/mo covers cart abandonment, product recommendations, purchase tracking, and unlimited automation at the lowest price available. For complex behavioral automation at scale with deep Salesforce/CRM integration: ActiveCampaign, but budget accordingly.

---

### Coach / Consultant / Online Educator

**Winner: GetResponse**

GetResponse's Creator tier ($69/mo annual ~$56.58) bundles email + webinars + course creator + sales funnels + website builder — tools that would cost $200–400/mo purchased separately. Zero transaction fees on courses. Caveat: confirm the Marketer tier ($59/mo) rather than Starter if you need more than 1 automation workflow.

---

### Enterprise / B2B with Sales Team

**Winner: ActiveCampaign**

The CRM, deal pipeline, lead scoring, Win probability, and Salesforce bidirectional sync make ActiveCampaign the only platform in this comparison with serious sales-team integration. Budget for Professional tier ($49/mo annual at 1K contacts, scaling significantly with list size) and a dedicated user to manage the platform.

---

### True Beginner (Just Starting, No Technical Background)

**Practical choice: Mailchimp Essentials** (for the familiar interface and tutorial ecosystem) — but acknowledge the 4-step automation limitation immediately and plan for Standard upgrade. Alternatively, Kit's free tier is simpler than Mailchimp's Standard and more powerful than Mailchimp's free plan.

---

### Budget-Conscious Business Wanting Real Automation

**Winner: Moosend — unambiguously**

$7/mo for unlimited sends, unlimited automation workflows, heatmaps, and e-commerce tracking. The next closest is Mailchimp Standard at ~$20/mo with send caps and no heatmaps.

---

### Nonprofit Organization

**Winner: Moosend**

25% nonprofit discount stackable with 20% annual discount = approximately 40% off Pro pricing. At 5,000 contacts, Pro would be approximately $19/mo after combined discounts. Verify discount availability directly with Moosend.

## Bottom Line

There is no single best email marketing platform. There is a best one for your specific situation.

| Situation | Use This |
|-----------|----------|
| Building a personal brand / newsletter | **Kit** |
| E-commerce on a budget | **Moosend** |
| Webinars + courses + email combined | **GetResponse** |
| B2B with a sales team | **ActiveCampaign** |
| Absolute beginner, just need to start | **Kit (free)** or **Mailchimp (free)** |
| Already on Mailchimp and frustrated with pricing | **Moosend** |

The one platform this roundup recommends against as a primary recommendation: **Mailchimp for anyone past the 250-contact free tier**. The automation limitations on Essentials, the Trustpilot score pattern, the inflating contact billing, and the pricing escalation make it a poor value for most growing businesses. Its only clear wins are brand name recognition and native SMS.
