## Kit's Three Tiers

Kit offers three plans: **Free**, **Creator**, and **Creator Pro**. Each tier is designed around a specific stage of the creator journey.

---

## The Free Plan: 10,000 Subscribers, No Charge

This is the headline that surprises most people evaluating email platforms. Kit's free plan supports up to **10,000 subscribers** at no cost. For context, Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with 1,000 sends per month. Kit's free tier is not a trial — it's a permanent plan you can stay on indefinitely.

**What the Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited broadcasts (one-time email sends)
- Basic subscriber management
- Embeddable signup forms
- One landing page
- Basic email templates
- Community support

**What the Free plan does NOT include:**
- Automated email sequences
- Visual automation builder
- Third-party integrations (no Shopify, no Zapier, no native CRM connects)
- A/B testing
- Priority support

The Free plan is genuinely useful for building your list and sending newsletters. It is not sufficient for running automated funnels, course delivery sequences, or any workflow that requires triggers and conditional logic. If you need automation at all, you need Creator.

## Who Kit Is Best For

**Kit is an excellent fit if you are:**

- A **newsletter writer** building an audience from scratch — the free tier up to 10,000 subscribers is unmatched
- A **course creator or coach** who needs automated delivery sequences for digital products
- A **blogger or content creator** who wants a clean, subscriber-centric email experience without enterprise bloat
- Starting out with fewer than 10,000 subscribers and want zero cost until you're generating revenue
- A **service business or consultant** who communicates primarily through nurture sequences and broadcast newsletters

**The honest catch:** At scale, Kit gets expensive relative to alternatives:

| Platform | 25,000 Subscribers | 50,000 Subscribers |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Kit Creator | $199/month | $379/month |
| Moosend Pro | $160/month | $315/month |
| Mailchimp Standard | ~$400/month | ~$700/month |

At 25,000 subscribers, Kit costs $39/month more than Moosend for similar automation depth. At 50,000 subscribers, the gap widens to $64/month — nearly $770/year. For high-volume senders or businesses without a strong "creator" identity, Moosend's pricing model becomes increasingly attractive at scale.

**Kit is less ideal if you:**
- Need SMS marketing (Kit does not offer it)
- Are an e-commerce brand running heavy promotional volume — Kit is optimized for sequences and nurture, not bulk broadcast
- Have complex multi-team workflows with many seats — Creator plan allows only 1 extra seat; Pro is unlimited but the cost climbs fast

Kit's free tier up to 10,000 subscribers is the most generous in the market, and the Creator plan's visual automation builder is genuinely excellent for the price point at lower subscriber counts. Above 25,000 subscribers, the math starts to favor Moosend for cost-conscious businesses that don't need Kit's creator-specific features.
