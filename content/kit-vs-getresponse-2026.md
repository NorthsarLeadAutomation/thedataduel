# Kit vs. GetResponse: Which Is Better in 2026?

Two strong platforms. One decision. Let's make it easy.

Kit (formerly ConvertKit) and GetResponse both serve email marketers, but they were built with different users in mind. Kit was designed for individual creators. GetResponse was designed for growing businesses. The right choice depends on which world you live in.

This comparison breaks down what actually matters: features, pricing, automation depth, and real-world use cases.

---

## The Quick Answer

Kit is better for solo creators, bloggers, and course sellers who want a clean, simple experience. GetResponse is better for businesses, marketing teams, and anyone who needs serious automation, webinars, or advanced analytics — at a better price per contact.

---

## Who's Each Platform Built For?

### Kit

Kit was founded by Nathan Barry, a creator himself, and it shows. The platform is optimized for newsletter writers and bloggers, course creators and digital product sellers, coaches and consultants, and solo operators who want simple, clean sequences.

Kit's philosophy is simple and creator-first — designed to feel less like enterprise software and more like a tool built for real people.

### GetResponse

GetResponse has been in the email marketing space since 1998 and has evolved into a comprehensive marketing platform. It serves small to mid-sized businesses, marketing teams, ecommerce brands, agencies managing multiple clients, and anyone who needs more than just email.

## Email Automation

### Kit Automation

Kit's automation is visual and straightforward. You build sequences using a canvas-style interface. Triggers include form submissions, link clicks, tags, and purchases. The logic is clean but intentionally simplified — it's built for creators who don't want to think about complex branching.

What Kit does well: simple visual sequence builder, tag-based subscriber management, broadcast emails for newsletters, and commerce integrations with Stripe and Gumroad.

What's missing: deep conditional logic, multi-channel automation (SMS, push), lead scoring, and complex ecommerce triggers.

### GetResponse Automation

GetResponse's automation builder is a full visual workflow engine. You can create complex multi-step funnels with conditional logic, scoring, tags, and triggers based on emails opened or clicked, website page visits, purchases and cart abandonment, custom events via API, SMS interactions, and webinar attendance.

The depth here is substantially greater. If you've ever wanted to build automations that rival what agencies charge thousands to set up, GetResponse's builder gets you there.

GetResponse wins — significant advantage in automation depth and flexibility.

---

## Email Design and Templates

### Kit

Kit's design philosophy is minimalist. Templates are text-heavy by default, which is actually ideal for personal newsletters that feel like they came from a real person, not a marketing department. Open rates often benefit from this.

The downside: if you want visually rich, designed emails with lots of imagery and custom branding, Kit is limiting.

### GetResponse

GetResponse offers 150+ professional templates with a full drag-and-drop editor. The AI email writer can generate subject lines, body copy, and call-to-action suggestions. Templates range from minimal text-based to highly visual.

The winner depends on your goal. Kit wins for a personal newsletter feel. GetResponse wins for designed marketing emails.

---

## Landing Pages

### Kit

Kit includes basic landing page creation — squeeze pages, opt-in forms, and simple sales pages. They're functional but not particularly flexible.

### GetResponse

GetResponse's landing page builder is comprehensive: 200+ templates, A/B testing, integration with ecommerce and webinar tools, mobile-responsive designs, and pop-up forms with exit-intent overlays.

GetResponse wins. The landing page feature is genuinely powerful and eliminates the need for tools like Leadpages or Unbounce.

---

## Webinars

Kit has no native webinar feature.

GetResponse has built-in webinar hosting for up to 1,000 attendees, along with automated webinars, on-demand replays, and webinar-triggered email sequences. This alone can justify the cost if you'd otherwise pay for a separate webinar platform.

GetResponse wins — Kit simply doesn't have this.

---

## Creator Commerce

This is where Kit fights back.

Kit has native digital product selling, paid newsletters, and Stripe integration built in. You can sell a course, an ebook, or paid community access directly through Kit without needing a separate tool.

GetResponse integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, and ecommerce platforms, but doesn't offer the same native creator commerce experience.

Kit wins here. If selling digital products is your core business, Kit's native commerce features are smooth and well-designed.

---

## Deliverability

Both platforms are reputable. Independent testing (EmailToolTester 2025 data):
- GetResponse: ~88–92% inbox rate
- Kit: ~88–91% inbox rate

Both are solid. GetResponse has a slight edge in some independent studies, but neither is a concern.

---

## Integrations

Kit has strong native integrations for creators: Teachable, Gumroad, Stripe, Shopify, Podia, and more. Zapier covers everything else.

GetResponse has broad business integrations: Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, Facebook, Google Ads, and extensive Zapier and Make support.

Kit wins for creator tools; GetResponse wins for business and ecommerce tools.

---

## Support and Learning Resources

Both offer email and chat support on paid plans. GetResponse's knowledge base is extensive, with tutorials for complex automations. Kit has a strong creator-focused community and learning resources specifically for bloggers and online course creators.

Both are adequate. The better option depends on your context.
