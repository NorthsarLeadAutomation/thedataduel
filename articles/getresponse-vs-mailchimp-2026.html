GetResponse vs Mailchimp: Which Is Better in 2026?

You've narrowed it down to two options: GetResponse or Mailchimp. Both are well-known, both have millions of users, and both promise results. So which one actually wins for your business in 2026?

This isn't a puff piece. We're going to break down pricing, features, deliverability, and real use cases so you walk away knowing exactly which tool to use.

The Quick Answer

GetResponse is the better overall value for small to mid-sized businesses, especially those focused on email automation, webinars, and list growth. Mailchimp remains the easiest entry point for total beginners but gets expensive fast and has lost ground on automation depth.

Now let's prove it.

GetResponse wins. At almost every tier, it costs significantly less and includes more features. Mailchimp's pricing scales aggressively, and the free plan's limitations make it a stepping stone, not a long-term home.

Email Automation

This is where the gap widens considerably.

GetResponse Automation

GetResponse has a visual workflow builder that lets you build multi-step sequences triggered by tags, behaviors, purchases, link clicks, page visits, and more. You can build complex funnels that rival much more expensive platforms.

  • Drag-and-drop automation builder
  • If/then/else conditional logic
  • Tagging and lead scoring
  • Ecommerce triggers (abandoned cart, purchase follow-ups)
  • SMS and web push in workflows

Mailchimp Automation

Mailchimp added automation years ago but it's still catching up. The Customer Journeys feature works for basics but lacks the depth of GetResponse's conditional logic. You'll hit walls quickly if you're building anything beyond a welcome sequence.

  • Customer Journey builder (improved but limited)
  • Basic triggers (signup, purchase, date-based)
  • Limited branching logic
  • No native webinar or SMS integration

GetResponse wins here, and it's not close for anyone serious about automation.

Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp has historically had an edge here. Their template library is extensive, modern-looking, and easy to use. Their drag-and-drop editor is polished.

GetResponse's templates have improved significantly in 2025–2026 and the editor is now genuinely competitive. The included AI email generator can write subject lines and body copy, which saves real time.

Mailchimp wins on aesthetics; GetResponse wins on built-in AI tools. Call it a tie overall.

Deliverability

Both platforms are reputable senders with strong infrastructure. Independent studies (EmailToolTester 2025) consistently show:

  • GetResponse: ~88–92% inbox rate
  • Mailchimp: ~85–90% inbox rate

GetResponse edges ahead, particularly for transactional and high-volume sends. Mailchimp has faced some deliverability issues tied to its shared IP pools and abuse on their free plans.

GetResponse wins by a slight margin.

Landing Pages and Lead Capture

GetResponse includes a full landing page builder on all paid plans. You can build squeeze pages, sales pages, and opt-in forms without any third-party tool.

Mailchimp has basic landing pages but they're limited in design flexibility and don't support A/B testing on lower plans.

GetResponse wins. The built-in landing page tool is genuinely useful.

Webinars

GetResponse is one of the only email platforms with a built-in webinar feature. If you run live trainings, product demos, or community calls, this alone can justify the price — you're avoiding a separate Zoom or WebinarJam subscription.

Mailchimp has no webinar feature.

GetResponse wins.

Ease of Use

Mailchimp wins here. It's genuinely simpler to get started. The UI is clean, the onboarding is friendly, and you can send your first email in under 10 minutes.

GetResponse has a steeper learning curve, especially around the automation builder. But once you know it, it's powerful.

Mailchimp wins for beginners who just want to send emails quickly.

Integrations

Both platforms integrate with major tools: Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, Zapier, Make, CRM tools, webinar platforms, and ad networks.

Mailchimp has a slight edge in the number of native integrations. GetResponse covers all the essentials and adds its own built-in tools to compensate.

Mailchimp wins marginally on integration breadth.

Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

  • You're brand new to email marketing and want the simplest possible start
  • You have fewer than 500 contacts and want a free-forever option
  • Your team is non-technical and will struggle with automation builders
  • You only need basic newsletters, no funnels

Who Should Choose GetResponse?

  • You're ready to invest in email automation and want real results
  • You want landing pages, webinars, and automations under one roof
  • You're scaling past 1,000 contacts and Mailchimp's pricing is painful
  • You run a digital product, course, or coaching business
  • You want better deliverability at scale

Final Verdict

GetResponse wins in 2026. For the price, you get more features, better automation, stronger deliverability, and tools that actually support business growth. Mailchimp is easier to start with, but you'll likely outgrow it — and the pricing jump when you do is significant.

If you're serious about email marketing, GetResponse is the smarter long-term investment.
