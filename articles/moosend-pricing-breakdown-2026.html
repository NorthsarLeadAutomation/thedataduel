How Moosend Counts Subscribers (vs. Mailchimp)

This distinction is important enough to call out directly.

Moosend bills based on active, subscribed contacts only. Unsubscribed contacts, bounces, and suppressed addresses do not count toward your tier. If you have 10,000 people in your system but 3,000 have unsubscribed, you're paying for 7,000.

Mailchimp, by contrast, counts all contacts in your audience — including people who have already opted out — unless you manually archive them. This leads to situations where Mailchimp users are paying for 10,000 contacts while only being able to email 6,000–7,000 of them.

At scale, the billing approach Moosend uses can represent a 20–40% cost difference — on top of Moosend's already lower base rates.

Moosend Enterprise

For organizations above 50,000 subscribers, or those with specific requirements around dedicated IPs, custom SLAs, SSO, or advanced account management, Moosend offers a custom Enterprise plan.

Enterprise pricing is negotiated directly and typically includes:

  • Dedicated account manager
  • Custom sending infrastructure
  • Priority onboarding
  • Advanced user permissions
  • Custom integrations

Most small and mid-size businesses won't need Enterprise. The Pro tier handles most use cases at substantial scale. Enterprise becomes relevant when you're managing multiple brands, require contractual deliverability guarantees, or have compliance requirements around data residency.

Who Moosend Is Best For

Moosend fits well if you are:

  • A small or mid-size business that sends email regularly and wants predictable, subscriber-honest billing
  • A high-volume sender who needs unlimited sends without watching email quotas
  • Currently on Mailchimp and feeling sticker shock — especially at 5,000+ contacts
  • Building marketing automations (drip sequences, behavioral triggers, cart abandonment) without paying for a higher-tier plan to unlock them
  • A service business or agency that needs landing pages bundled in without a separate tool subscription

Moosend is less ideal if you:

  • Run a Shopify store deeply integrated with Mailchimp's revenue tracking — Moosend's e-commerce integrations are improving but not as mature
  • Need SMS marketing — Moosend does not currently offer SMS; you'd need a separate tool
  • Are primarily a creator or newsletter writer building a community — Kit's subscriber-centric features (referrals, subscriber scoring, creator network) are better suited for that use case
  • Have fewer than 500 subscribers and want to start completely free with full features — Kit's free tier supports up to 10,000 subscribers

The Bottom Line on Moosend Pricing

Moosend's pricing model is built around a simple principle: you pay for people you can actually email. No inflated contact counts, no per-email charges, no feature gates that force you into a higher tier to access automation.

At 10,000 subscribers, Moosend Pro costs $88/month with full features included. The equivalent Mailchimp Standard plan — before factoring in unarchived unsubscribes, potential Mandrill fees, or duplicate audience counts — lists at $135/month.

For budget-conscious businesses that send consistently and want access to the full automation and analytics toolset from day one, Moosend Pro is one of the best-value email marketing platforms available in 2026.
