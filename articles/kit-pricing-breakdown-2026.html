Kit (ConvertKit) Pricing 2026: Full Breakdown for Creators and Newsletter Writers

Northstar may earn a commission if you purchase through affiliate links in this content, at no extra cost to you.

Kit's Pricing Model Is Built for How Creators Actually Grow

Most email marketing platforms price against you. You start small, get hooked on the product, then watch your costs scale aggressively as your audience grows. Kit (formerly ConvertKit) took a different approach: a genuinely useful free tier up to 10,000 subscribers, followed by paid plans that add capability — not access to features you already assumed were included.

This is the full Kit ConvertKit pricing breakdown for 2026: every tier, every price, what you get, and where it stops making sense.

Kit's Three Tiers

Kit offers three plans: Free, Creator, and Creator Pro. Each tier is designed around a specific stage of the creator journey.

The Free Plan: 10,000 Subscribers, No Charge

This is the headline that surprises most people evaluating email platforms. Kit's free plan supports up to 10,000 subscribers at no cost. For context, Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with 1,000 sends per month. Kit's free tier is not a trial — it's a permanent plan you can stay on indefinitely.

What the Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited broadcasts (one-time email sends)
  • Basic subscriber management
  • Embeddable signup forms
  • One landing page
  • Basic email templates
  • Community support

What the Free plan does NOT include:

  • Automated email sequences
  • Visual automation builder
  • Third-party integrations (no Shopify, no Zapier, no native CRM connects)
  • A/B testing
  • Priority support

The Free plan is genuinely useful for building your list and sending newsletters. It is not sufficient for running automated funnels, course delivery sequences, or any workflow that requires triggers and conditional logic. If you need automation at all, you need Creator.

Creator Plan: Automation Unlocked

The Creator plan is where most working creators and small businesses land. It includes everything in Free, plus the automation and integration layer that makes email marketing actually powerful.

Creator plan adds:

  • Automated email sequences (drip campaigns)
  • Visual automation builder (if/then branching flows)
  • Third-party integrations: Shopify, WooCommerce, Teachable, Zapier, and 100+ others
  • Unlimited landing pages
  • Remove Kit branding from landing pages and forms
  • 1 additional team member seat

The visual automation builder is the defining feature. It lets you build conditional logic flows — "if subscriber clicks link A, send sequence B; if not, wait 3 days and send sequence C" — without touching a line of code. For course creators and coaches, this is the infrastructure for your entire customer journey.

Creator Pro adds over Creator:

  • Newsletter referral system — Built-in referral tracking to grow your list via word-of-mouth rewards (no third-party tool needed)
  • Subscriber scoring — Engagement-based scores so you can identify your most active subscribers and segment accordingly
  • Facebook custom audiences sync — Automatically sync subscriber segments to Facebook for paid retargeting
  • Advanced reporting — Enhanced analytics including subscriber growth over time, deliverability breakdowns
  • Priority support — Faster response times
  • Unlimited team member seats

The newsletter referral system alone is worth the Pro premium if your growth strategy relies on organic sharing. Platforms like SparkLoop charge separately for this functionality; Kit builds it in.

Who Kit Is Best For

Kit is an excellent fit if you are:

  • A newsletter writer building an audience from scratch — the free tier up to 10,000 subscribers is unmatched
  • A course creator or coach who needs automated delivery sequences for digital products
  • A blogger or content creator who wants a clean, subscriber-centric email experience without enterprise bloat
  • Starting out with fewer than 10,000 subscribers and want zero cost until you're generating revenue
  • A service business or consultant who communicates primarily through nurture sequences and broadcast newsletters

The honest catch: At scale, Kit gets expensive relative to alternatives:

Platform 25,000 Subscribers 50,000 Subscribers
Kit Creator $199/month $379/month
Moosend Pro $160/month $315/month
Mailchimp Standard ~$400/month ~$700/month

At 25,000 subscribers, Kit costs $39/month more than Moosend for similar automation depth. At 50,000 subscribers, the gap widens to $64/month — nearly $770/year. For high-volume senders or businesses without a strong "creator" identity, Moosend's pricing model becomes increasingly attractive at scale.

Kit is less ideal if you:

  • Need SMS marketing (Kit does not offer it)
  • Are an e-commerce brand running heavy promotional volume — Kit is optimized for sequences and nurture, not bulk broadcast
  • Have complex multi-team workflows with many seats — Creator plan allows only 1 extra seat; Pro is unlimited but the cost climbs fast

The Bottom Line on Kit Pricing

Kit's free tier up to 10,000 subscribers is the most generous in the market, and the Creator plan's visual automation builder is genuinely excellent for the price point at lower subscriber counts. The platform is built specifically for creators, newsletter writers, and anyone selling knowledge or services — not as a generic bulk-send tool.

The value proposition is strongest below 10,000 subscribers where the free plan is viable, and between 1,000–10,000 where Creator's $29–$119 pricing is competitive. Above 25,000 subscribers, the math starts to favor Moosend for cost-conscious businesses that don't need Kit's creator-specific features.
